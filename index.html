Abstract

The rapid emergence of airborne platforms is enabling new forms of aerial surveillance, which offers significant advantages in terms of scale, manoeuvrability, deployment speed, and vulnerabilities. These platforms play a vital role in areas such as aerial monitoring of specific targets, sites, or activities and information gathering. However, conventional vision sensors often struggle in rapidly changing environments due to the bandwidth-latency tradeoffs. High frame rates require large bandwidth and lower frame rates can cause significant delays, making them less effective, especially on platforms with limited resources. To address this, we explore the use of event cameras that can overcome such limitations. In this work, we use a high-resolution event camera onboard an airborne platform. We develop a novel unsupervised approach for motion segmentation of any moving object, eliminating the need for manual labelling. We tested our approach on various datasets, showcasing its ability to handle different types of motion and various numbers of moving objects effectively. The evaluation shows state-of-the-art results compared with existing approaches.
